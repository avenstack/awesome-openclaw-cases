## What it does
- Uses a fixed content calendar (for example weekday themes) to draft platform-specific posts.
- Sends drafts to the owner for approval before publishing, with optional timeout-based auto-publish.
- Publishes on schedule and supports follow-up engagement windows after posting.

## Pain Point
Growth teams and solo operators struggle to post consistently. Content creation, scheduling, and distribution compete with core business work, causing irregular posting cadence and weak audience recall.

## Core value of this case
This case turns social posting into an operational pipeline: planned themes, scheduled execution, and controlled approval. It increases posting consistency while keeping brand risk manageable through review gates.

## Typical scenarios
- Founder-led businesses that need regular posting without hiring a full content team.
- Multi-platform weekly posting plans (X/LinkedIn) with minimal manual overhead.
- Campaign periods where daily visibility matters but team bandwidth is limited.

## How to setup
1. Define channel targets, weekly themes, tone, and prohibited topics.
2. Configure draft generation and publishing schedules (for example evening draft + morning publish).
3. Add review routing and fallback publish logic for low-risk content streams.

## FAQ
### Should all posts require manual approval?
Not necessarily. Keep approval for high-risk channels and allow low-risk recurring formats to auto-publish.

### How is posting consistency improved in practice?
By predefining themes and schedules, content creation becomes a recurring workflow instead of ad hoc decisions.

### How do we reduce brand risk with automation?
Set explicit tone/topic rules, keep prohibited-topic filters, and route sensitive content through human review.
