## What it does
- Scans recent Reddit and X discussions to surface repeated user complaints and unmet needs.
- Structures findings into ranked pain points and opportunity signals.
- Feeds selected pain points into an MVP build workflow inside OpenClaw.

## Skills You Need
- [Last 30 Days](https://github.com/mvanhorn/last30days-skill/)

## Pain Point
Teams often guess what to build and validate too late. This pattern moves discovery forward by grounding decisions in current user conversations before investing engineering time.

## Core value of this case
- Reduces idea risk by using real-world demand signals.
- Connects research output directly to build actions.
- Shortens the path from insight to prototype.

## Typical scenarios
- Finding startup ideas from active user frustration.
- Prioritizing feature backlog with external demand evidence.
- Running weekly opportunity scans in a target niche.

## How to setup
1. Install and enable the Last 30 Days skill in your OpenClaw environment.
2. Prompt OpenClaw to research your target topic over the last 30 days on Reddit and X.
3. Ask for ranked pain points, common requests, and opportunity gaps.
4. Select one pain point and trigger an MVP build request around that specific problem.

## FAQ
### Is this only for startup founders?
No. It is also useful for existing products that need continuous discovery from public user feedback.

### Why use social sources instead of only surveys?
Because social platforms capture spontaneous and often unfiltered pain points that formal surveys may miss.

### Does this automatically guarantee a good product idea?
No. It improves idea selection quality, but product success still depends on execution and differentiation.
