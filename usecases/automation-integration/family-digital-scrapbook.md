## What it does
- Scans images sent via messaging (photos, artwork, school projects)
- Converts images into structured journal entries
- Uses shared family calendar to infer location, attendees, and event context
- Provides a secure web app for grandparents and family members to view and comment
- Maintains a living digital scrapbook of family memories

## Pain Point
Many families want to document their children's growth—artwork, school projects, and favorite moments—but face several challenges:

- Photos and artwork pile up on phones with no easy way to organize them
- Physical artwork gets lost or damaged over time
- Grandparents and extended family miss out on daily moments
- Maintaining a consistent record requires ongoing effort that's hard to sustain
- Memories lack context (when, where, who was there) when revisiting them later

This is fundamentally a memory preservation and family connection problem, not just a photo storage issue.

## Core value of this case
A family digital scrapbook automates the tedious parts of memory preservation while enhancing family connection. The direct value includes:

- Capture memories as they happen: send photos in the moment instead of batch-uploading later
- Automatic context enrichment: calendar integration adds "where, when, who" without manual entry
- Family inclusion: grandparents and distant relatives stay connected through the web interface
- Lower barrier to participation: kids actively remind parents to "send to the bot," making it a shared family activity
- Preservation: digital format protects artwork and photos from physical loss

## Typical scenarios
- Parents sending photos of their kids' artwork throughout the day
- Sharing school projects and achievements with extended family
- Creating a chronological record of family activities and events
- Allowing grandparents to follow daily moments despite distance
- Building a searchable family archive with automatic tagging
